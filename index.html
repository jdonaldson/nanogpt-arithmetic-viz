LAYER 0

L0 — Three regions of the (min, max) triangle

For mul, L0 is the (min, max) triangular sub-lattice — operands encoded as an unordered set. Swap pairs (a, b) and (b, a) collapse to within 8% of random-pair distance, so 100 raw pairs project to ~55 distinct spots.

The cluster has three regions, each corresponding to one edge or interior of the triangle:

  • Zero arm (u_2 ≈ +1.2 to +2.0): pairs with min = 0. (0,0) sits at the V-vertex, (0,5) at the apex of the arm, (0,9) on the far edge.
  • Diagonal arm (u_2 ≈ 0): square pairs with min = max. (1,1), (5,5), (9,9) sweep along this arm with u_1 growing by magnitude.
  • Off-diagonal interior (u_2 ≈ −0.8 to −1.2): pairs with both operands nonzero AND unequal — (2,7), (3,5), (4,6), (3,7). This is where the bulk of products 6..30 live.

Within each region, u_1 tracks magnitude: small products on the left, large on the right. (2,7)=14 and (3,5)=15 are nearly identical neighbors here because their products differ by 1; in raw prod space they're indistinguishable from each other but very far from (8,8)=64. In log space they're closer to the magnitude of (8,8) — log(15)=2.71 vs log(64)=4.18, only a 1.5 spread vs 49 raw.

Try log_prod: u_2 jumps from R²=0.00 against raw prod to R²=0.23 against log. The log-magnitude axis is the early signature of L0's role as the architecture's log encoder — block 0's MLP decodes log(a+1) and log(b+1) at R²=0.99 by L1 (causally established via activation patching).

And sign_class is degenerate for mul/sub at L0 — for mul it's just does the answer equal 0?, which is the same as zero_flag (19 grey + 81 red).

Same starting geometry as mul — L0 is operator-agnostic. Joint R² of (u_1, u_2) onto (min, max) = 0.79 for add, the strongest of any operator.

u_1 loads on prod (R²=0.85), min (0.82), sum (0.79). u_2 picks up abs_diff (R²=0.48) and max (0.36). For add, sign_class only marks one point grey — (0, 0), the single pair where a + b = 0 — so coloring by sign_class makes everything else red. Try zero_flag instead to see the 19 pairs where one operand is zero (those sit along the upper arm of the V).

Same starting geometry — sub's L0 has no signed information at all: signed_diff has R² = 0.000 on every eigenvector. Sign emerges downstream.

u_1 loads on prod (0.86), min (0.85), sum (0.79). u_2 picks up zero_flag (0.52). Recolor by sign_class: positive (red) and negative (blue) answers are completely overlapping at L0 — the model hasn't decided yet which is which.

LAYER 1

L1 — Magnitude composite refines (mul-specific)

Caveat first: this is mul's view alone. The operator dispatcher story applies to cross-operator patching (block-1 attention causally flips operator with ≥91% rate). But we're holding the operator fixed at mul, so that flip doesn't appear here as a visible basis change.

Within mul, L1 looks similar to L0: u_1 still tracks magnitude — max (R²=0.81), sum (R²=0.73), prod (R²=0.45). The cluster has slightly rotated; you can see this by scrolling back to L0 and watching the morph. u_2 has flattened (no single feature dominates) — L1 is consolidating L0's signal, not yet reorganizing it.

Add's L1 looks similar to L0 because addition's combination rule (a + b) is symmetric in (a, b) — no need to break operand symmetry yet. u_1 ≈ max (0.81), sum (0.69), and the (min, max) joint R² is still 0.56.

Caveat: the cross-operator dispatcher fires at block 1 (causal via patching), but within add's single-op view it doesn't appear as a basis flip — the dispatch keeps add's computation symmetric.

This is the dramatic L1 case. Sub IS the only anti-commutative operator (a − b ≠ b − a), so its downstream machinery has to know which operand is the subtractor. At L1, u_1 = b with R² = 0.78 — the subtractor has been isolated as a coordinate.

a only loads at R²=0.00; max at 0.42. Sub's L1 is selectively pulling out one operand's identity. Compare to mul/add at L1, where neither a nor b individually dominates — only their symmetric combinations do.

signed_diff is still R²=0.07 here; the sign answer itself emerges later.

LAYER 2

L2 — u_1 commits to zero_flag

By L2, u_1 ≈ zero_flag with R² ≈ 1.0. The Laplacian's leading non-trivial mode (the Fiedler vector) is now the cut separating zero pairs from non-zero pairs. Cluster topology has shifted: 2D-LATTICE → HUB. All three operators do this at L2 — shared substrate.

u_2 still carries magnitude — sum (R²=0.55), prod (R²=0.53). Click those to see how the non-zero cluster is ordered along magnitude even while zero pairs split off.

u_1 ≈ zero_flag (R² = 0.99). Same Fiedler cut as mul and sub. Add will not use this — addition's answer is sum, not a zero/non-zero classification. But the substrate is here.

u_2 carries max (R²=0.55) and sum (0.55) — magnitude already on the second axis, ready to take over at L3.

u_1 = b still (R²=0.69), but zero_flag is building up on u_2 (R²=0.17) and other axes. The shared L2 zero-detection event is happening, but sub's b-axis from L1 hasn't been overwritten yet.

signed_diff has crept up to R²=0.17 on u_2 — sign information is starting to appear.

LAYER 3

L3 — Annihilator gutter consolidates

u_1 stays on zero_flag (R² = 1.00) — same Fiedler structure as L2. For mul, the commit-class fork is zero-vs-nonzero — this is exactly the annihilator gutter direction (d′=8.5 at L3), now found unsupervised as the manifold's principal direction.

u_2 carries sum / prod / max: the non-zero pairs are now ordered along their magnitude/output direction, preparing for digit selection downstream.

u_1 shifts to sum (R²=0.71); add's leading mode now tracks the answer directly. zero_flag drops to u_2 (R²=0.36) — secondary, not primary.

This is add's operator-specific commit: skip the zero detection branch, go straight to the magnitude axis.

u_1 = signed_diff with R² = 0.76. Joint R² for (min, max) collapses 0.65 → 0.05. The leading plane has shifted decisively off the symmetric basis. u_2 picks up zero_flag (R²=0.76).

This is sub's operator-specific commit. Recolor by sign_class — the three answer-sign clusters are now visible.

LAYER 4

L4 — Commit-class MLP writes

L4's MLP injects +1.57 along the correct-digit direction for L4-commit pairs (typical, non-zero). Non-zero pairs spread into output sub-blobs along sum / prod. The zero cluster (color by zero_flag) stays tightly isolated — its commit happened at L3.

u_1 still on sum (R²=0.71). Pairs spread along a 1D-leaning ramp — answers 0..18 ordered along the principal coordinate. Recolor by prod to see the secondary spread (correlated with sum, but distinct).

Refinement of the signed_diff axis (R²=0.71). u_2 picks up max (0.36) and abs_diff (0.22) — distance from the a=b diagonal stretches each arm. Positive, zero, and negative answer classes tighten into distinct clusters.

LAYER 5

L5 — Linear contrastive digit selector

L5's MLP writes +6 on the correct digit row and −0.7 on each other. Effective rank ~10 = the 10 digit rows. (u_1, u_2) shows the coarse zero_flag split; digit identity now lives in u_3..u_10 — recolor by prod to see that within the non-zero cluster, magnitude organizes the spread.

For the cleanest read on what L5 has committed to: recolor by predicted_char. Each point's color is the actual next character the model emits at the = position — distribution: 19× 0, 18× 1, 15× 2, ..., 3× 9. Mul never predicts a non-digit (unlike sub).

u_1 ≈ sum / prod composite (~0.6). Add's L5 looks more continuous than mul's because sum doesn't quantize into discrete commit classes — answers form a 1D ramp, not a simplex. Color by max to see the secondary spread on u_2.

Recolor by predicted_char to see the digit-class structure. Add's prediction distribution is heavily skewed: 47 of 100 pairs have answer ≥ 10, so the first emitted char is 1 (the tens digit). Pairs predicting 1 form one big cluster; the rest spread across 0–9.

Between/within cluster ratio = 10.5×. The three sign_class classes form a 2-simplex — the neural-collapse / ETF pattern. u_1 ≈ signed_diff (R²=0.74); u_2 ≈ abs_diff (R²=0.24).

Recolor by predicted_char for the semantic kicker: the three vertices correspond exactly to three predicted-next-character classes — the model emits − at the negative vertex (45 pairs), 0 at the zero vertex (10 pairs), and digits 1–9 at the positive vertex (45 pairs). Sub is the only operator that ever emits a non-digit; mul and add never do.

Within the positive vertex, individual digit identity is in u_3+: L5 has effective rank ~10 (matching the 10 digit rows of the unembedding head), with the higher eigenvectors carrying digit-specific structure. In 3D you can rotate to see whether u_3 starts pulling out that digit structure.
